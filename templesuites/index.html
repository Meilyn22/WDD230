Featured Temple

Announcement: 15 November 1968

Groundbreaking and Site Dedication: 7 December 1968 by Hugh B. Brown

Public Open House: 17 September–2 November 1974

Dedication: 19–22 November 1974 by Spencer W. Kimball

Public Open House: 28 April–11 June 2022

Rededication: 14 August 2022

Site:52 acres | 21.0 hectares

Exterior Finish: Reinforced concrete sheathed in 173,000 square feet of Alabama white marble

Architectural Features: Six attached spires with an angel Moroni statue

Ordinance Rooms: Six instruction rooms, fourteen sealing rooms, and one baptistry

Total Floor Area: 160,000 square feet | 14,864 square meters

The temple was dedicated in 1974 after an open house that attracted over 750,000 people, including several international dignitaries. The temple was the first temple built by the church east of the Mississippi River since 1846, when the original Nauvoo Temple was dedicated.
